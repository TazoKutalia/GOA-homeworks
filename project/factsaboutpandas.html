Information about Pandas

panda, is a bear species endemic to China. It is characterised by its black-and-white coat and rotund body. The name "giant panda" is used to distinguish it from the distantly related red panda.

Adult individuals average 100 to 115 kg (220 to 254 lb), and are typically 1.2 to 1.9 m (3 ft 11 in to 6 ft 3 in) long. The species is sexually dimorphic, as males are typically 10 to 20% larger. The fur is white, with black patches around the eyes, ears, legs and shoulders.

Giant pandas have adapted larger molars and expanded temporal fossa to meet their dietary requirements.

Habitat

The giant panda is exclusively found in six mountainous regions in a few provinces. It is also found in elevations of up to 3,000 m (9,800 ft).

They once lived in lowland areas, but farming, forest clearing and other development now restrict giant pandas to the mountains.

Diet

Its diet consists almost entirely of bamboo, making the bear mostly herbivorous, despite being classified in the order Carnivora.

Pandas evolved the ability to effectively digest starch, making the shoot an important energy source.

Though mostly starch, bamboo shoots are 32% protein, and therefore a good substitute for a carnivorous diet.

Interesting Facts

Pandas have an extended wrist bone that functions like a thumb and helps them grip bamboo.

They are solitary animals, with males and females typically coming together only during the breeding season.

Pandas have a slow reproductive rate, with females being able to conceive only for a few days each year.

Conclusion

Pandas are iconic and beloved animals, often seen as symbols of wildlife conservation efforts worldwide.
